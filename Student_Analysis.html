Key Insights from Student Performance Analysis

Test preparation matters the most Students who completed the test preparation course consistently scored higher across all subjects.

Parental education has noticeable influence Students whose parents had higher education levels showed better academic outcomes on average.

Lunch type correlates with performance Students receiving standard lunch performed better than those on free/reduced lunch, indicating possible socioeconomic effects.

Reading and writing scores are strongly correlated Students who do well in reading usually also perform well in writing.

Gender differences are small While there are slight variations, overall performance differences between genders are not drastic.

Conclusion

This analysis explored factors influencing student academic performance using exam score data.

The study revealed that preparation courses and parental education levels play a key role in determining outcomes. Socioeconomic indicators such as lunch type also show meaningful influence. Strong correlations between reading and writing scores suggest that literacy skills are interconnected.

These findings suggest that schools can improve student outcomes by encouraging preparation programs, supporting literacy development, and addressing socioeconomic barriers.
